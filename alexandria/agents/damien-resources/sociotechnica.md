# SocioTechnica — company context

> Optional context for Damien. Load before any strategy, positioning, audience,
> or voice decision. This is who we are and why we're doing this.

## What SocioTechnica is

SocioTechnica is a two-person company — Danvers and Jess — operating on almost no
resources ("with our hands tied behind our backs") and building toward a vision
of **AI as colleagues**: humane automation where humans live and work alongside
AI agents. The work is **real software, not strategy decks**. The center of
gravity is **Alexandria**, the underlying system, surrounded by a growing and
still-evolving suite of tools — **Playmaker Studio, the Director Cockpit,
Hearthfire**, and the distinctive **"talking coins"** interface for AI
colleagues, among others. (That list isn't complete or current — there's more in
the repo and more being built than any one card captures.)

The founders' own framing of why they're doing this: **"living a beautiful life
and sharing it with others"** — and, concretely right now, **earning a seat at
the table inventing the future**. Alan Kay is the archetype: not rich, but with
a real seat at the table. The goal isn't unlimited wealth — **"enough is
enough."**

## Danvers and Jess — their roles

- **Danvers** is a full-fledged IC and technical producer. He's the one
  "coding," shipping the actual software (Playmaker Studio, the Director Cockpit,
  Hearthfire, Alexandria) that extends what SocioTechnica can do — and he's also
  the strategist who lays out the plan and stress-tests it.
- **Jess** is the relationship and grounding side of the partnership: deeply
  networked in the future-of-computing and tools-for-thought communities, the
  one who keeps the strategy concrete ("boil it down for my simple concrete
  brain"), and who currently holds the partnership's economic floor by working
  at Elicit half-time.

They describe themselves as complementary "aliens" — they work well together
precisely because they think differently. Both are firmly committed to staying
in **North Carolina**; the price to relocate them "has gone up."

## The goal: getting resourced

Stripped to its essence, the near-term objective is to **change their economic
posture — to get resourced** — so they can keep building this rather than walk
away with nothing. The plan is a deliberately flexible **"check-down" pyramid of
leverage**, with the same underlying pitch tailored per audience: *"imagine if
you were doing this with these guys."* (See `campaign-strategy.md` for the full
pyramid and timeline.)

## Values and principles

A few throughlines govern how they work:

- **The beautiful-life north star.** Work hard, but not at the cost of the life;
  resources are a means, not the end ("enough is enough," Alan Kay over a
  venture-scale moonshot).
- **A strong preference for independence.** The private aim even in a fundraise
  is to keep control and "build our lab on the lake," not to chase billion-dollar
  outcomes.
- **A flexible, hypothesis-driven posture.** Everything is "the plan as stated,"
  held loosely ("changing this plan could be the smartest thing we do"), with a
  sharp eye on the **Next LX failure mode** of over-investing in a path "to no
  avail."
- **A documented operating discipline.** A human approval gate — AI drafts and
  does the grunt work (even running future board meetings), but humans decide and
  send — and a taste-driven insistence on building what they genuinely want, even
  the weird parts ("we will not remove the coins").
