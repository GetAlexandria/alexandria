## What New Media means now

**New Media is a software-mediated, AI-augmented system for creating,
distributing, measuring, and monetizing content and community across digital
channels — where media assets behave like product surfaces and audience
relationships behave like owned infrastructure.** The unit of work is not "a
video" or "a newsletter" but a software-enabled content object that can be
versioned, remixed, localized, measured, distributed, and monetized across
surfaces.

Its converging dimensions:

- **Media + software** — content organized by software logic; one asset becomes
  many managed outputs.
- **AI** — native to creation, editing, repurposing, search, personalization,
  and the interface itself, not a side tool.
- **Automation** — automate the repetitive parts; keep high-judgment decisions
  human.
- **Community** — audiences are reactors, redistributors, collaborators, and
  co-creators; community is a production input and retention engine.
- **Creator economy** — recognizable voices, portable formats, direct
  audiences, diversified revenue.
- **Distribution** — a core competency: owned channels, cadence, syndication,
  platform-native discovery, measurable conversion.
- **Monetization** — designed at the format/channel level: subscriptions,
  sponsorships, premium tiers, commerce, community, licensing, product revenue.
- **Productization** — editorial insight becomes tools, features, and
  interactive experiences ("media as product, product as media").
- **Metrics** — instrumented end to end; tie value to a leading (north-star)
  metric, not vanity output.

## The Executive Producer of New Media

The senior operator who **designs, runs, and scales the company's multi-format
media system** across editorial, creative, product, AI, and growth. Owns the
path from **idea → distribution → measurable outcome**, and ensures media is a
durable strategic asset, not filler.

### Core responsibilities (the full team's surface area)

| Area | What the EP owns | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Editorial & creative direction | Format mix, content slate, launch narratives, series architecture, talent packaging, quality bar | Media is brand and strategic leverage |
| Production system | End-to-end workflows for video, audio, text, social, events, AI-native experiences | Scale comes from system design, not heroics |
| AI & automation | Tooling stack, workflow automation, repurposing, human-review checkpoints, provenance | Changes throughput and cost; introduces trust risk |
| Distribution | Owned channels, platform strategy, syndication, search, social, community loops, launch playbooks | "Own your distribution" is table stakes |
| Monetization | Sponsorships, subscriptions, premium formats, events, product upsell, lead-gen | New media has to compound commercially |
| Product integration | Turning recurring editorial demand into tools and interactive experiences | The frontier is media↔product |
| Community | Audience habit, fan participation, expert networks, creator partnerships, feedback capture | Retention and ideation infrastructure |
| Measurement | KPI framework, dashboards, experiments, postmortems, north-star alignment | Output without measurement is noise |
| Governance | AI policy, editorial standards, disclosure, rights, safety, platform compliance | Where teams avoid long-run brand damage |

- **Editorial Lead** — writers and researchers; thesis, narrative, quality.

Story judgment · production leadership · product thinking (assets as UX/funnels/

## Operating principles

- **Treat media as a product system.** One idea → a primary asset + structured
  derivatives (essay, clip, podcast excerpt, quote, social post, community
  thread, email prompt, and — when warranted — an interactive/software layer).
- **Own at least one durable channel.** Platform reach is useful; owned habit is
  what compounds.
- **Use AI to compress cycle time, not erase judgment.** Disclose meaningful AI
  contribution; keep human review for facts, taste, brand, and final publish.
- **Design for reusability.** Package source, prompts, workflows, and learnings
  so the next asset gets cheaper and better.
- **Cadence matters.** A sustainable, compounding cadence beats occasional hero
  launches.
- **Authenticity over volume.** Beware mass-produced/repetitive output that
  erodes trust or platform eligibility — "fake efficiency" is the top failure
  mode.

## Recommended process

Signal sensing → thesis & angle → primary-format choice → production brief →
AI-assisted drafting & research → human edit & fact review → multi-format
packaging → distribution by channel → community response → analytics &
postmortem → reusable assets/prompts/playbooks → (back to thesis).

## Governance checklist

- Define allowed AI uses by workflow stage (ideation, drafting, summarization,
  translation, editing, personalization, publishing).
- Require human review for factual claims, sensitive/reputational statements, and
  final publishing.
- Disclose AI use when it materially contributes to final output.
- Protect originality/authenticity; avoid mass-produced or repetitive output.
- Protect input data and IP; use role-based permissions for powerful AI features.
- Keep a provenance trail: source docs, prompts, editors, approvals, disclosures.
- Red-team for impersonation, brand-risk outputs, hallucinations, rights issues.
- Review platform monetization/eligibility policies periodically.

## KPIs worth tracking

North-star metric · audience growth by owned channel · returning-audience rate ·
conversion to owned audience · watch time / completion · repurposing yield ·
subscriber or lead conversion per flagship asset · revenue per content franchise
· production cycle time · error/correction rate.
