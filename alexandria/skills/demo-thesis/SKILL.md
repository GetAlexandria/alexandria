# Demo Thesis

Produce the strategic brief that all later demo-video work depends on.

Read `references/demo-thesis-process.md` unless the user explicitly asks for a
very quick pass. If product context is missing, make the smallest useful
assumptions and label them.

## Workflow

1. Gather the available source material: product context, Raven knowledge,
   launch goal, customer objections, competitor frame, and product capabilities.
2. Define one target viewer precisely enough that the video can make tradeoffs.
3. Write the before/after belief change in one sentence.
4. Choose one demo promise and no more than 3-5 proof requirements.
5. Mark each proof requirement as product-visible, evidence-backed, or
   explanation-only.
6. Define the primary CTA and the scope boundary: what this demo is not trying
   to prove.
7. Run the quality checks from the reference before presenting the brief.

## Output

Use this structure:

```text
Demo Thesis Brief

Product:
Target viewer:
Viewer context and pain:
Current alternatives:
Category frame:
One-sentence demo promise:
Viewer belief before watching:
Viewer belief after watching:
Primary CTA:
Scope boundary:

Proof requirements
| proof | why it matters | burden | current support | risk |

Terms to use:
Terms to avoid:
Open questions / assumptions:
Hand-off notes for Story Spine:
```

Keep the output decision-ready. Do not drift into a full script, shot list, or
product click path; those belong to Story Spine and Demo Path.
