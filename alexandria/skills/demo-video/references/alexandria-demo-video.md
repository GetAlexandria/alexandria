# Alexandria Demo Video Reference

Use this as a starting point until a project-specific brief exists. Damien's
first three stations are `demo-thesis`, `story-spine`, and `demo-path`; do not
skip ahead to recording material until those artifacts hold together.

## Default Audience

Product and engineering leaders who need product context to survive handoffs,
agent work, and implementation churn.

## Default Takeaway

Alexandria turns scattered product source material into durable context that
agents and workflows can use without repeatedly re-asking the director.

## Demo Arc

1. Start with a project that has raw product source material but no durable
   agent context.
2. Show Raven gathering and banking Vision context.
3. Show the Playbook as the place where Fabro workflows become runnable product
   plays.
4. Show the agent bench as the visible roster of product-context agents.
5. Close on the next artifact the director wants, such as a demo script,
   decision brief, or workflow-ready plan.

## Review Questions

- Who is the video for?
- What product surface must be visible on screen?
- Which claims need source material or a live workflow run?
- What artifact should exist by the end of the demo?
