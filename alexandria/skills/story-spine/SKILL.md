# Story Spine

Turn the demo thesis into a watchable narrative structure with proof logic.

Read `references/story-spine-process.md` unless the user explicitly asks for a
very quick pass. If no Demo Thesis exists, create a compact inferred thesis
first and label it as a draft assumption.

## Workflow

1. Import the target viewer, demo promise, belief change, proof requirements,
   CTA, and scope boundary from the Demo Thesis.
2. Draft 2-5 hook options that match proof the product can actually show.
3. Choose one causal story shape and build a 5-8 beat outline.
4. Map every major claim to visible product proof, external evidence, or a cut.
5. Draft a rough script or run-of-show only after the beat logic holds.
6. Read the spine as if spoken aloud: remove jargon, long setup, and orphaned
   feature hops.
7. Produce hand-off notes for Demo Path: required product moments, visuals,
   audio, and risks.

## Output

Use this structure:

```text
Story Spine

Narrative objective:
Chosen format:
Hook options:
Selected hook:

Beat outline
| beat | purpose | viewer shift | claim | proof needed |

Claim-proof map
| claim | why it matters | visible proof | scene | risk if not shown |

Rough run-of-show:
Lines to avoid:
Required product moments:
Open questions / assumptions:
Hand-off notes for Demo Path:
```

Do not let the story follow product architecture by default. The order should
follow viewer comprehension and proof, not menu structure.
