# Story Spine Process

Story Spine is station 2 in Damien's software-product demo process. Its artifact
is a beat outline plus a claim-proof map. It turns the Demo Thesis into a
causal, watchable sequence.

## Inputs

- Demo Thesis brief
- Target viewer and belief-change map
- Demo promise
- Proof requirements
- Primary CTA
- Product capabilities, screenshots, workflows, or known product moments

## Process

1. Import the thesis and define the narrative objective.
2. Draft hook options that surface viewer stakes quickly.
3. Choose the format: live demo, voiceover, conversation, documentary, launch
   film, narrated screen capture, or hybrid.
4. Build a 5-8 beat arc: setup, tension, product action, proof, transformed
   state, CTA.
5. Map every major claim to proof.
6. Remove, weaken, or label claims that cannot be shown or sourced.
7. Draft a rough run-of-show or script only after the claim-proof map holds.
8. Read the spine aloud and cut jargon, slow setup, and disconnected features.

## Beat Outline Template

```text
Proof beat 2:
Proof beat 3:
Outcome / transformed state:
Primary CTA:
Secondary CTA:
```

## Story-Spine Lens

Use this as a drafting lens, then adapt it for product truth:

```text
Once upon a time...
Every day...
But one day...
Because of that...
Because of that...
Because of that...
Until finally...
And ever since then...
```

## Quality Checks

- Can the story be summarized in one sentence?
- Is there a reason to keep watching after the first 10-15 seconds?
- Does the hook match proof the product can later show?
- Does every important claim have proof or an explicit downgrade?
- Does every beat create tension, prove value, clarify the product, or move the
  viewer toward the CTA?
- Is the CTA built into the spine rather than bolted on?

## Failure Modes

| Failure | What it looks like | Correction |
|---|---|---|
| Weak opening | The video warms up before earning attention. | Draft multiple hooks and pick the one tied to real proof. |
| Random feature hopping | The script sounds like a changelog. | Force causal beats and a claim-proof table. |
| Telling instead of showing | Benefits are narrated but not visible. | Mark proof scenes and cut unsupported claims. |
| Product-architecture order | The story follows menus instead of viewer comprehension. | Reorder around belief change and proof. |
| CTA drift | The ending asks for something the video did not earn. | Reconnect CTA to transformed state. |

## Done When

The story is causal, proof-backed, and ready to hand to Demo Path. Every major
beat should tell the Demo Path station what the product must show.
